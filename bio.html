BIOGRAPHY

"Let's play two!"

I am regarded as one of the greatest baseball players of all time. I played for the Chicago Cubs Cubs in the 50s and 60s and was the first black player on the team.

After growing up in Dallas, Texas, I was drafted into the U.S. Army and served during the Korean War. Shortly thereafter, I signed with the Cubs and quickly became one of the best players for the franchise.

I have now been inducted into the Baseball Hall of Fame, had my number 14 retired by the Cubs and received the Presidential Medal of Freedom.
